About ICLET 2025

The International Conference on Libraries and Emerging Technologies for Smart Knowledge Ecosystems (ICLET 2025) explores the profound impact of new technologies on the information landscape. These advancements are reshaping the way information is developed, processed, preserved, and disseminated, transforming entire ecosystems. Libraries—public, academic, and special—are adapting through significant reengineering to meet the dynamic needs of users in a rapidly evolving digital age.

ICLET 2025 provides a platform for experts, practitioners, and educators to engage in meaningful discussions about the future of libraries. We invite technologists, implementers, and trainers to join us in shaping the future of information systems.

Why Join Us

The International Conference on Libraries & Emerging Technologies (ICLET 2025) offers a unique platform for academics, researchers, professionals, and technology innovators to:

  • Engage with Global Experts
  • Explore Cutting-edge Innovations
  • Network and Collaborate
  • Enhance Professional Skills
  • Contribute to Sustainable Futures

Whether you’re a librarian, technologist, policymaker, or student — ICLET 2025 is your opportunity to be part of a global movement redefining knowledge ecosystems.

Conference Tracks

Track 1: AI and Machine Learning in Library Services
  • Intelligent discovery systems
  • AI-driven reference and recommendation services
  • Predictive analytics for user behavior and resource management
  • Ethical implications of AI in libraries
Track 2: Smart Libraries and Knowledge Ecosystems
  • Smart infrastructure and sensor technologies
  • Interoperability in smart environments
  • Internet of Things (IoT) applications in libraries
  • Green technologies and sustainable practices
Track 3: Digital Transformation and Library Innovation
  • Library automation and workflow optimization
  • Digital library architecture and cloud-based services
  • Emerging digital tools for curation and preservation
  • Libraries as Smart Learning Hubs
Track 4: Policy, Leadership, and Strategic Planning
  • National and institutional policies on emerging technologies
  • Strategic foresight and scenario planning
  • Human-computer interaction in library systems
  • Open data, FAIR principles, and research data management
